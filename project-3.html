Todotastic!

Learning Django through building a contemporary classic: a to-do app.

Project Overview

This was the first app I created after starting to learn Django, as I wanted to have a good idea of how to engineer a straightforward Create, Read, Update and Delete (CRUD) software app in Django.

I wanted to go above and beyond the well-established basic operations, so I added extra functionality, such as giving a to-do a due date - with a warning sparked when it goes overdue - grading to-dos in terms of importance, and I made both a progress bar and stats for the home to-do list page. It also supports multiple users, with authentication and email confirmation on signup.

There were some new challenges posed by my choice to use the Bootstrap user interface framework with Django, such as adding custom classes to Djangoâ€™s Form elements, as well as overriding the default colour scheme.

One of my favourite behaviours in the app is how users can mark a to-do as important, urgent or give one to-do critical priority - and if they decide to mark another to-do as critical instead, the app will automatically downgrade the previously critical to-do.
